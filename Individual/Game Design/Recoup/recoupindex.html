Context of the project


Ever since I started self-taught web development, I decided I wanted to practice what I have been learning with my game development/design background. The game will be a collection of minigames with a story that ties them all together. So far I have done some of the minigames in vanilla Javascript, but I will eventually assemble them all using Phaser 3.

Description


Recoup (working title) is a story of grieving. A man who loses his loved one during a car accident. Now he's dealing with his emotions and trying to move on with his life.



Game design choices


-Each minigame will represent metaphorically one of the five stages of grief of the KÃ¼bler-Ross model.

-First level: Denial. | Metaphorical game: Matching pairs game
Clinging to the objects of the person the main character's used to love. Comparing the actual new absent reality to the "better" one it once was.

-Second level: Anger. | Metaphorical game: Whack-a-mole
Directing the frustration towards the items that remember the main character's loss.

-Third level: Bargaining. | Metaphorical game: Nokia Snake clone
Enduring multiple conditional situations (every decision of where to move in order not to clash with your own self)

-Fourth level: Depression | Metaphorical game: Space Invaders clone
Isolation. Dealing with the inner demons of solitude.

-Fifth level: Anger. | Metaphorical game: Tetris clone
Recovering. Putting all the pieces together.

-Final level: Anger. | Metaphorical game: Frogger clone
Moving up and on, after finding peace and acceptance.
